The Hitman: A Tale of Rohit Sharma, Captain Marvel of Indian Cricket

Rohit Sharma, the name evokes awe, fear, and pure cricketing genius. The man with the Midas touch, capable of conjuring magic with the willow, stands tall as the captain of the Indian cricket team across all formats. But his journey, like his batting, has been anything but conventional. From the streets of Nagpur to the pinnacle of world cricket, Rohit's story is one of resilience, talent, and an unyielding desire to conquer. He wasn't your quintessential prodigy, but a late bloomer who honed his skills on concrete pitches, his talent nurtured by the legendary Dronacharya awardee, Ramakant Achrekar. His international debut in 2007 was a glimpse of things to come. A century on debut, against South Africa, announced his arrival. But inconsistency plagued him, and whispers of "potential unfulfilled" began to echo. However, Rohit, the fighter, persevered.
